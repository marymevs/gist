Your day, on paper.

Gist is a two-page broadsheet — printed, set in serif type, waiting on your desk before you wake. Calendar, weather, the six emails that actually need you, the people you can’t lose track of, and a quiet page to think on. No feeds. No app to open. No reason to reach for the phone.

Six apps before coffee — or one page.
The problem

You wake up and reach for the phone.

Inbox, calendar, Slack, news, weather, three group chats. Six apps before coffee, and somehow you still feel behind. The signal lives somewhere in there — but the noise gets to you first, and most mornings the noise is what you remember.

The promise

One page. Printed. Already on your desk.

Each morning, at the minute you choose, Gist prepares an edition for an audience of one. Your schedule for today, the weather where you are, three world stories that fit who you are, the inbox threads that are waiting on you, the people you said matter — and a second page that doesn’t ask anything of you. Sent to your printer by email. Waiting before you’re awake.

How it works

Three minutes to set up. Forever after, it just arrives.

01
Tell Gist who you are.

Your name, your role, the people who matter to you, anything you’re counting down to. The brief reads like it was made for you because it was.

02
Connect Calendar and Gmail.

Read-only OAuth. Gist never sends mail on your behalf, never writes to your calendar. We use them to know your day, not to act on it.

03
Forward to your printer.

Most modern printers have an email-to-print address — HP ePrint, Brother iPrint, Epson Email Print. Add a Gmail filter and your edition prints itself, every morning, before you’re awake. Or read it on the web at /today.

Why paper

Paper holds your attention. Screens take it.

The phone wants you on it forever. A printed page can’t notify you, can’t autoplay, can’t refresh, can’t ping. It tells you what you need to know — then it stops. You finish reading and you’ve got your whole day back.

Gist isn’t anti-tech. It’s the polite shape technology should take. We do the screen work for you. You get the paper.
